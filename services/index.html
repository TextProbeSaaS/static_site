Open-Ended Questions

Open-ended questions play a crucial role in eliciting spontaneous responses and avoiding biases in survey data. As highlighted by Reja et al. (2003), open-ended questions allow individuals to express their opinions freely, without being influenced by predefined answer options. We incorporate open-ended questions strategically to capture authentic insights and perspectives from survey participants.

Close-Ended vs. Open-Ended Questions in Web Questionnaires

Close-ended questions limit respondents to predefined answer choices, potentially biasing their responses. In contrast, open-ended questions encourage genuine engagement and provide valuable insights into unanticipated perspectives. By combining both question types strategically, we ensure comprehensive data collection while minimizing biases in survey results.
