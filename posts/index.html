    High Frequency Polling Summary

    Sun, Feb 25, 2024 - 300 Words
    Summary This study explores the potential of online surveys, even those with skewed demographics, to accurately measure public opinion. The authors demonstrate their method by analyzing an Xbox poll of US voters in the 2012 election. By applying statistical adjustments, their estimates align closely with actual results, suggesting non-representative polls can be surprisingly accurate, offering advantages like speed, cost, and real-time tracking of public sentiment. Key Points The paper is about using non-representative polling, such as opt-in online surveys, to accurately and continuously track public opinion, with proper statistical adjustment.
