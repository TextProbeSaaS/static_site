Unleashing the Power of Open-Ended Questions in Surveys: A TextProbe Perspective

In the realm of survey research, the debate between open-ended and close-ended questions has long been a topic of discussion. Over the years, numerous studies have highlighted the richness of responses garnered from open-ended questions, contrasting them with the more constrained nature of close-ended queries. Authors such as Comley (1996), Gonier (1999), and Kwak and Radler (1999), among others, have underscored the depth and spontaneity of insights derived from open-ended surveys conducted through email and web platforms.

One seminal work, “Open-ended vs. Close-ended Questions in Web Questionnaires” by Reja et al., provides significant insights into the distinct advantages of open-ended questions. The study not only emphasizes the value of capturing spontaneous responses but also highlights the importance of mitigating potential bias introduced by predetermined answer choices. However, it also acknowledges the challenges associated with open-ended surveys, such as the need for extensive coding and higher item non-response rates.
